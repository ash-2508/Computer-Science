India's contribution to satellite technology.

India's space technology projects, such as the GSAT series and NavIC, have helped the country become a leader in space technology.

  • Data obtained from meteorological satellites is used to track cyclones, asses their intensity, make landfall predictions, and forecast extreme weather events. This data is then used for monitoring disaster events and assessing damages,predict weather patterns, generate disaster-related products, and drive ecosystem models.

  • Meteorological satellites, also known as weather satellites, are Earth observation satellites that monitor the weather and climate. They collect data on the atmosphere, including temperature, moisture, winds, and cloud cover.

  • Kalpana-1 India's first dedicated meteorological satellite, launched in 2002. It was originally known as MetSat-1.

  • INSAT-3D Launched in 2013, this satellite is equipped with an imaging system and atmospheric sounder. It provides enhanced meteorological data and observations.
